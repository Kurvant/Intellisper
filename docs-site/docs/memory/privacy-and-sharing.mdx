This page is the definitive statement of who can see your personal memory. It matters: personal memory
holds your data, and the rules below are the contract for how it's protected.

The short version: **your personal facts are private, and stay private unless you and your admin both
deliberately open a narrow, per-fact door — which you can close again at any moment.**

## The three-condition gate

A personal (**My memory**) fact can be seen by a workspace admin **only when all three** of these are
true at once:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Your admin has unlocked sharing for the workspace">
    An administrator has turned on the workspace-wide sharing switch. Until they do, no personal fact is
    visible to anyone but its owner — no matter what individual members choose.
  </Step>
  <Step title="You have opted in to sharing">
    You — the fact's owner — have turned on your personal *opt in to admin visibility* setting. This is
    your choice alone; an admin cannot make it for you.
  </Step>
  <Step title="You have marked that specific fact as shared">
    Sharing is per-fact. Even when the first two conditions hold, only the facts you've individually
    marked **shared** are visible. Everything you leave **private** stays private.
  </Step>
</Steps>

All three are required. Remove any one — the admin locks sharing, you opt out, or you set the fact back
to private — and the fact is no longer visible to anyone but you.

```text
Visible to an admin  ⟺  workspace sharing unlocked
                     AND you opted in
                     AND this fact is marked SHARED
```

Facts in **org** and **flow** scope are shared by their nature (that's what those scopes mean), so they
aren't governed by this gate — it applies specifically to personal facts.

## Private is an absolute veto

Marking a fact **private** — or simply leaving it private, which is the default — is a veto that nothing
overrides:

- An **admin cannot** mark your fact as shared. Only you can change your own fact's visibility.
- An **admin cannot** opt you in to sharing on your behalf.

So a private personal fact is unreachable by anyone else, regardless of the workspace sharing switch or
your opt-in state. The only facts an admin can ever see are ones **you** marked shared, while **you** are
opted in, while sharing is unlocked.

## Revocation is immediate

The three conditions are checked **live**, every time facts are read — they're not baked in when you
share. That means revocation takes effect on the very next read:

- Turn off your opt-in → your shared facts vanish from admin view immediately.
- Set a fact back to private → it's hidden immediately.
- Your admin re-locks workspace sharing → all shared facts across the workspace are hidden at once.

Turning sharing back on later restores visibility of exactly the facts that still meet all three
conditions — nothing more. Revoking never destroys your data or your per-fact marks; it only changes what
others can see, right now.

## What an admin can and can't do

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="An admin CAN">
    - Unlock (or re-lock) workspace-wide sharing.
    - View facts that meet all three conditions.
    - See a count of how many personal facts are currently shared.
  </Card>
  <Card title="An admin CANNOT">
    - Mark any member's fact as shared.
    - Opt a member in to sharing.
    - See any fact a member left private.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

See [Admin governance](/memory/admin-governance) for the admin side, and [Settings](/memory/settings) for
your opt-in and per-fact controls.
