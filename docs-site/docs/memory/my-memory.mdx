**My memory** is your personal store of facts — preferences, context, the details you'd otherwise have
to repeat. It is **private to you by default**: only you can read it, and only you can curate it. No other
member of your workspace, and no admin, can see a personal fact unless you have deliberately chosen to
share it (see [Privacy & sharing](/memory/privacy-and-sharing)).

## What lives here

Personal facts are the things that make the agent and the Copilot feel like *yours* — "I prefer metric
units," "my main project is the Q3 launch," "always format dates as ISO." When [auto-capture](/memory/settings)
is on, the agent saves facts like these as it works; you can also add them by hand.

## How it's used

- The **agent** recalls your relevant personal facts at the start of a task, so it starts with your
  context instead of a blank slate.
- The **Studio Copilot** does the same for in-app help.

Both only happen when [auto-recall](/memory/settings) is on. Personal memory is never used by flow steps
— those run without a specific user, so they can only reach org and flow memory.

## Curating it

You are always in control of your own facts:

- **Add** a fact by hand.
- **Edit** a fact's content.
- **Delete** a fact (["forget" it](/memory/export-and-delete)).
- **Mark** a fact shared or private — your personal veto over whether it can ever be visible to an admin.

Your ability to **edit and delete** your own facts is never blocked by hitting a storage limit — only
saving *new* facts is capped. You can always curate down.

:::note Born private
Every new personal fact starts as **private**. It only becomes shareable if you explicitly mark it so —
and even then, sharing requires two more conditions to be met (see [Privacy & sharing](/memory/privacy-and-sharing)).
:::
