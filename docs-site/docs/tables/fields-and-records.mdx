A table is made of **fields** (its columns) and **records** (its rows). Fields define the shape of your
data; records are the data itself.

## Field types

Each field has a type that determines what it holds:

| Type | Holds |
| --- | --- |
| **Text** | Free-form text. |
| **Number** | Numeric values. |
| **Date** | Calendar dates. |
| **Dropdown** | One of a fixed set of options you define. |

You define a table's fields when you create it, and you can add or change fields as your needs evolve.
A **dropdown** field carries the list of allowed options, so records in that column are limited to the
choices you've set.

## Records

Records are the rows of your table. A record holds one value per field.

You can:

- **Add** records — one at a time or in bulk.
- **List** records, paginated, to browse or fetch your data.
- **Update** a record's values.
- **Delete** records you no longer need.
- **Clear** the whole table to remove every record while keeping the fields.

Records can be managed by hand in the app, through the [API](/api-reference/intellisper-documentation), or
by your [flows](/flows/building-flows) as they run.

## Exporting

You can **export** a table to get its data out, and you can **save a table as a template** to reuse its
field structure for a new table later.
