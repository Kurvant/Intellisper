Tables give you a place to store structured data inside Intellisper — like a spreadsheet, but wired into
your automations. A table has **fields** (columns) and **records** (rows), and your [flows](/flows/building-flows)
can read from and write to it as part of an automation.

Use tables to hold the data your automations work with: a list of leads to process, a log of results, a
lookup of settings, a queue of items to handle.

## Working with a table

- **Create** a table and define its fields.
- **Add, edit, and delete records** — by hand, or from a flow.
- **Export** a table's data.
- **Clear** a table to remove all its records while keeping its structure.
- **Save a table as a template** to reuse its structure elsewhere.

Tables are scoped to your project, so each project has its own tables.

## Tables and flows

Tables are most powerful when your automations use them. A flow can look up a record, append a row of
results, or update a status as it processes work — turning a table into the shared state your automation
reads and writes across runs. And with [table webhooks](/tables/table-webhooks), a change to a table can
itself trigger a flow.
