Your plan determines which features are included and how much of each you can use. This page explains how
plans and their limits work; the per-feature limits themselves live on each feature's own page.

## What a plan controls

A plan controls two kinds of things:

- **Whether a feature is included** — some capabilities (like the [Agent](/agent/overview) and
  [Memory](/memory/overview)) are paid features that a plan either includes or doesn't.
- **How much you can use** — for metered features, your plan sets monthly allowances and storage caps.

## How limits behave

Two conventions apply across the product:

- A limit of **zero means the feature isn't included** on that plan. When you try to use it, you'll see an
  **upgrade prompt** rather than an error — it's an invitation to add the feature, not a failure.
- **Unlimited** means there's no cap on that feature.

When you reach a monthly allowance, further use of that feature is paused until the next month (or until
you upgrade) — again surfaced as an upgrade prompt.

## Products and bundles

Intellisper's paid capabilities — Studio (flows), the Agent, and Memory — can be bought on their own or
bundled. Memory in particular is a separate entitlement: a Studio-only workspace can add memory to power
its [Copilot](/assistants/copilot) and flows without needing the agent. See each feature's plans page for
what its tiers include.

:::info Some allowances are still settling
A few bundled allowances reflect current shipped behaviour and may change as pricing is finalised. Each
feature's plans page notes where that's the case.
:::
