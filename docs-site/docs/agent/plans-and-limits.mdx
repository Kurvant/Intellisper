The agent is a paid capability on **Cloud** and **Enterprise**. What's included, and how much you can do
each month, depends on your plan.

## The paid door

The agent is gated by a single switch on your plan. If the agent isn't included, opening it shows an
**upgrade prompt** rather than an error — it's an invitation to add the agent, not a failure. Once the
agent is on your plan, your tier sets the allowances below.

## Monthly allowances

Several agent activities are metered per month, pooled across your workspace. When you reach an
allowance, further actions of that kind are declined until the next month (or until you upgrade) — this
shows up as an upgrade prompt, the same as the paid door.

| Allowance | Free | Starter | Pro | Team | Enterprise |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Browser actions** | 25 | 400 | 3,000 | 3,000 | Unlimited |
| **Research fetches** | 3 | 40 | 300 | 300 | Unlimited |
| **File operations** | 3 | 50 | 500 | 500 | Unlimited |
| **Routine runs** | 50 | 2,000 | 20,000 | 20,000 | Unlimited |
| **Quick tools** (e.g. grammar) | 20 | 300 | 3,000 | 3,000 | Unlimited |

## Automation limits

[Batches and schedules](/agent/automation) have their own per-tier limits:

| Limit | Free | Starter | Pro | Team | Enterprise |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Max rows per batch** | — | 200 | 1,000 | 1,000 | 5,000 |
| **Concurrent rows** | — | 2 | 3 | 5 | 10 |
| **Schedules** | — | 5 | 20 | 40 | 200 |

On the Free tier, batches and schedules aren't included — a batch or schedule limit of zero means the
feature isn't on that plan.

Every plan also enforces fixed safety ceilings regardless of tier: a batch can never exceed 10,000 rows,
100 columns, or 2,000 characters per cell.

## Reasoning model

The agent uses a fast model for ordinary work and escalates to a stronger model when a task stalls. The
most powerful **reasoning** model is available only on **Pro, Team, and Enterprise** — Free and Starter
plans use the fast and escalation models but do not reach the top reasoning tier.

## How limits behave

Two conventions are worth knowing:

- A limit of **zero** means the feature isn't included on that plan — you'll be prompted to upgrade.
- **Unlimited** means no cap.

Intellisper is deliberately careful about how it enforces these: if the system can't confirm your
entitlements for a moment (for example, a transient database hiccup), it **holds back privileged
features** (like batches and the reasoning model) but **does not block your metered day-to-day actions**
— so a blip never locks a paying customer out of ordinary work.
